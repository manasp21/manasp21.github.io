The End of Intractability

Physics is facing a crisis of complexity, driven by a deluge of data and computational challenges that defy traditional methods. A new scientific pillar is rising to meet this challenge: Artificial Intelligence.

The Data Deluge & The Computational Wall

Our ability to generate data has outstripped our capacity to comprehend it. Simultaneously, the fundamental complexity of the universe presents a computational barrier that even exponential growth in computing power cannot scale.

The Great Deluge: Particle Physics

At the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), 40 million proton collisions occur every second. A brutal triage system filters this, saving only the most "interesting" events.

The Celestial Torrent: Astronomy

The Vera C. Rubin Observatory's LSST will scan the entire southern sky every few nights, generating a motion picture of the cosmos.

10,000,000

This firehose of discovery requires automated systems to identify novel and surprising events.

The Exponential Wall

The curse of dimensionality means the resources to simulate quantum systems grow exponentially, hitting a wall that Moore's Law cannot climb.

The Four Pillars of Science

Science has evolved. Alongside theory and experiment, computation arose as a third pillar. Now, facing new challenges, a fourth pillar is emerging: Machine Learning.

Pillar 1: Theory

Formulating elegant mathematical descriptions.

Pillar 2: Experiment

Testing theories against reality.

Pillar 3: Computation

Simulating systems with explicit instructions.

Pillar 4: Machine Learning

Inferring rules and patterns directly from data.
